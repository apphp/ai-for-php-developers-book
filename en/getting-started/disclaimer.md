# Disclaimer

#### **About the Nature of This Book**

This book is not an academic textbook at the level of Stanford University or the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. It is a practical engineering guide for developers – primarily those working in the PHP ecosystem.

The priority here is understanding principles and applying them in practice. Strict mathematical proofs are intentionally simplified when they interfere with intuitive understanding. If you are a researcher or a mathematician, some explanations may feel too informal – that is deliberate.

#### **On Simplifications and Limitations**

The book uses simplified models, synthetic datasets, and demonstration examples. Real-world data is always more complex – noisy, imbalanced, and often contradictory.

We do not fully dissect the internal mechanics of frameworks like TensorFlow or PyTorch, nor do we explore architectural details of specific models such as GPT-4. Instead, we focus on fundamental principles – probability, entropy, decision boundaries, attention, and next-token prediction.

#### **On Security and Ethics**

Topics such as phishing, adversarial attacks, prompt injection, and LLM security are discussed strictly for educational and defensive purposes.

Any attack examples are provided to help you understand how defensive strategies and security awareness approaches are built within companies. Responsibility for how this knowledge is applied lies with the reader.

#### **On Responsibility and Application**

This book is not legal, financial, or cybersecurity advice. Before integrating AI into a product or business process, you must consider your country's legal requirements, personal data regulations, and relevant industry standards.

All code is provided "as is" – for educational purposes. Before using it in production environment, it must be tested, optimized, and secured.

#### **On the Limitations of AI**

AI systems are probabilistic models. They make mistakes. They hallucinate. They reproduce bias present in their training data.

No model – even those created by companies such as OpenAI or Google – guarantees absolute accuracy.

Always add validation, monitoring, and human oversight.

#### **The Purpose of This Book**

This book is not about hype. It is about understanding.

If, after reading it, you stop seeing AI as magic and start seeing statistics, linear algebra, probability, and engineering trade-offs – then it has done its job.
